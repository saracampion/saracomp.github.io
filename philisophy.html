MY TEACHING PHILOSOPHY

I want to be a fun teacher that students are excited to learn from. I want to be able to foster a classroom environment that is both creative and inclusive. I want all of my students to feel like they can have fun in my classroom, whist maintaining safe classroom procedures, and also feel like they are able to succeed in all areas.

As Technology and Applied Studies (TAS) is a very practical area of learning, this encourages many students to choose TAS subjects as they get to be creative and make projects that they are able to take home and show their family and friends, projects that they are really proud of. TAS classrooms can be quite dangerous as students are handling tools and machinery that can cause significant injuries. To ensure students remain safe at all times, students must follow set safety procedures that are put in place otherwise they will be unable to participate in practical classes. TAS can also be quite a challenging subject as students may be unfamiliar with the tools and machinery used. This unfamiliarity can lead to a lack of engagement. To avoid this, students who are feeling particularly challenged would be provided with greater scaffolding to complete projects for example, explicit / step-by-step instructions so they feel less overwhelmed about what they need to do.
