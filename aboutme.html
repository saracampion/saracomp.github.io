ABOUT ME

Hello! I’m Sara Campion, a student teacher in my third year of university at Australian Catholic University. I am committed to fostering a classroom culture where every student has the potential to succeed and thrive. I am completing a double degree of a Bachelor of Education (Secondary) and a Bachelor of Arts (Design Innovation and Technologies). I have completed three teaching placements in both the government system and the Sydney Catholic School System. On my placements I have taught lessons that are engaging for all learners as I incorporated different teaching methods. Throughout my university study and on my teaching practicums I have developed units of work and lesson plans that have been beneficial for my teaching, so that I had structured lessons that were well paced and beneficial for student learning and engagement.

University Projects:

My major subject area is textiles, which I have a great passion in. I have been creating textile projects since I was about 8 years old, ranging from knitting, crochet, embroidery, hand sewing, and more recently creating garments for both myself and friends that have been worn to weddings, parties, and more. I have also produced leotards for myself for rhythmic gymnastics competitions.

At university I have completed 4 textiles units (one still in progress). The first unit aimed at a technology mandatory (year 7 & 8) level of sewing. In this unit we produce a pyjama set and a small tote bag. This class helped people to gain a basic set of textile skills and knowledge to be able to teach students in their future careers. The next two textile units I completed were aimed at a stage 5 level (year 9 & 10), where we produced two art quilts, and leather products. During the creating of the art quilt, we learnt different dying methods and surface embellishment techniques to produce decorative quilts that began as a white piece of fabric. The leatherwork unit was the first time I had worked with leather, so I gained a large amount of knowledge of the origins of leather products and how to work with leather. I then was able to produce a leather coin pouch/bag. I am currently undertaking my major unit aimed at a stage 6 level (year 11 & 12) in textiles where I have to produce a major project, similar to a HSC major project. I am producing a rhythmic gymnastics unitard. I have not yet developed this project, however, I am in the testing stage to develop samples of the elements that I intend to include in the final product. This testing allows me to refine my skills and techniques to ensure that the final product has a professional finish before putting it on display in the exhibition at the end of this semester.


Industrial Technology:

One of my minor subject areas is industrial technology, which includes both timber and metal work. Prior to starting my university studies, I had very minimal experience in industrial technology, the only experience being in year 7…which was roughly 10 years ago. Now that I have completed multiple units in this area, I have gain great confidence in the use of machines and various techniques that are involved in producing the projects in these subject areas.

The first unit in completed was aimed at a technology mandatory level (year 7 & 8), where we produced a pencil box, a soup ladle, a rubber band car, and a desk lamp. These projects were extremely helpful in refining the basics of timber and metal work so that I could successfully participate in further units. The next unit I completed was a timber unit, where I was able to expand my skills in wood working to successfully produce a chopping board, a stool, and an heirloom box. These three projects all use different techniques that I had never done before so I was able to broaden my knowledge of timber techniques. Initially I was nervous to complete these projects but once I got going, I had lots of fun making things that I could take home and show my family. I am currently undertaking a metal work unit. I feel very nervous in this unit as it is quite particular and everything must be exact, however my confidence is growing as the unit progresses. We are producing multiple projects including a hacksaw, a hammer, and a toolbox. These projects are still in the works, so I do not have any photos.


All of these projects that I have made in my studies are great teaching samples that I will be able to take into my future career and show my students what they could potentially produce as well.
